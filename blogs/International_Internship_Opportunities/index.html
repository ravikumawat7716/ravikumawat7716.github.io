Why Pursue International Internships?

Application Tips

1. Start Early

Many international internship applications open 6-12 months in advance. Create a timeline and set reminders for application deadlines.

2. Build a Strong Profile

  • Maintain a strong academic record (GPA above 3.0/4.0)
  • Participate in relevant projects and competitions
  • Contribute to open-source projects (especially for tech internships)
  • Develop a portfolio showcasing your work
  • Obtain strong letters of recommendation

3. Tailor Your Application

Customize your resume and cover letter for each opportunity. Research the organization and highlight relevant skills and experiences.

4. Prepare for Interviews

  • Practice coding problems (LeetCode, HackerRank)
  • Prepare for behavioral questions using the STAR method
  • Research the company and its recent projects
  • Prepare thoughtful questions for interviewers

5. Visa and Logistics

Start visa applications early, as processing times can vary. Many programs provide guidance and support for visa applications.

Funding and Scholarships

Additional Funding Sources: Many universities offer travel grants for international internships. Check with your career services office and academic department for funding opportunities.

  • Fulbright Programs: Various programs for US citizens
  • Benjamin A. Gilman International Scholarship: For US undergraduate students
  • DAAD Scholarships: For international students to study/intern in Germany
  • Erasmus+ Traineeships: For European students
